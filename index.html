Aromatic Aldehyde Synthesis

About team project

Aromatic Aldehydes

Toluene

to

Benzaldehyde

Commonly used in the flavor and fragrance industry

- Almond, vanilla, cherry, peach, spearmint flavoring;
- Flower and fruit scent;

Some used in manufacturing processes

- Insect repellants, pesticides, and fungicides;
- Plastic additive;
- Pharmaceutical precursors;

No viable industrial method to produce most derivatives; must be harvested from natural sources

- Industrial uses of many aromatic aldehydes have not been explored in depth for this reason;

Aromatic aldehydes have a wide range of useful applications, from flavors and fragrances to pharmaceutical precursors and plastic additives.

A large majority of these aldehydes are produced at low yield and over toxic catalysts.

This gives rise to the need to produce these molecules in a renewable, environmentally friendly, and high-yield manner.

Our project aims to meet these goals by developing a synthetic biology route to generate a library of aromatic aldehydes from their respective inexpensive toluene-based precursors that are an environmentally toxic waste in crude oil processing.

We utilize the xyl ortho pathway of Pseudomonas putida, which is cloned into Escherichia coli as a host platform. This pathway converts toluene derivatives with a wide range of functional groups in the meta and para positions on the ring to their corresponding aromatic aldehydes, leaving the meta and para substituents unaltered and therefore allowing for development of a library of products.

Aims

Our project is accredited to creating a library of aromatic aldehyde products from their respective toluene-based precursors from an inexpensive substrate.

To clone Escherichia coli as a host for expression

To convert toluene derivatives with a wide range of functional groups in the meta and para positions on the ring to their corresponding aromatic aldehydes

To incorporate current iGEM bioparts

To utilize the xyl ortho pathway from the pWW0 plasmid of Pseudomonas putida

To isolate the meta and para substituents for development of a robust library of products.
